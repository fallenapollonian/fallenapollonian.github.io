## Homicide

In high-income countries with more firearms, both men and women are at higher risk of homicide, particularly firearm homicide [(Hepburn & Hemenway, 2004)](https://files.catbox.moe/td3g80.pdf).

The US is among high-income countries a serious outlier when it comes to homicide, mostly due to their massive firearm homicide rates [(Grinshteyn & Hemenway, 2019)](https://repository.usfca.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1147&context=nursing_fac).

The presence of firearms increases aggression in people [(Benjamin et al., 2017)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1088868317725419).

A disproportionately high number of 5-14 year olds die from homicide, suicide, and unintentional firearm deaths in states and regions where guns are more prevalent [(Miller et al., 2002;](https://moscow.sci-hub.st/5262/a4a65f985a1236228760a36cbdc9d757/miller2002.pdf)  [Miller & Azrael, 2003)](https://files.catbox.moe/y36ppa.pdf).

In the US, higher levels of firearm ownership are associated with higher levels of firearm assault, firearm robbery, firearm homicides, and overall homicide [(Monuteaux et al., 2015)](http://ajpmonline.org/article/S0749-3797(15)00072-0/pdf).

Across developed countries, where guns are more available there are more homicides [(Hemenway & Miller, 2000)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11130511/).
